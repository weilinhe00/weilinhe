It is a great honor to attend the Anglo-German Summer School at the University of Oxford. Exploring the boundaries of AI and creativity with scholars from diverse backgrounds in such an academic atmosphere is incredibly exciting!

May 2026
Bristol, UK
Reviewer for the Spärck AI Scholarship at Bristol

It is a tremendous honor to take part as a reviewer at the University of Bristol for the Spärck AI Scholarship. The Spärck AI Scholarship is a highly prestigious, UK government-backed, fully funded scholarship for selected AI master's students at leading UK universities — it covers tuition fees and a tax-free stipend, and serves as strong recognition of each recipient's potential to contribute to the future AI workforce.

Being trusted to help select the next generation of AI researchers is both humbling and inspiring, and reading through the applicants' visions for the field has been genuinely uplifting. I am grateful for the opportunity to contribute, in a small way, to this initiative.

Apr 2026
Bristol, UK
Hosting the Wise Software Engineering Lab from Tianjin University

The Wise Software Engineering Lab from Tianjin University, led by Prof. Junjie Chen, visited the University of Bristol, and I had the pleasure of taking part in their visit.

During the visit, I had the privilege of listening to a talk by Prof. Yingquan Zhao, an Associate Professor at the College of Intelligence and Computing, Tianjin University, on his recent work in JVM testing. We had a wonderful discussion exploring the transferability of his methods to my current research direction on code generation, from which I benefited greatly.

Beyond the academic sessions, we took a trip together to one of Bristol's most iconic landmarks — the Clifton Suspension Bridge. The weather was absolutely lovely, and walking across Brunel's masterpiece together made for a memorable afternoon. Beyond the research exchanges, we also chatted about everyday life — everyone was humorous and easy-going, which made the whole experience genuinely enjoyable. Truly a delightful time!

In the photo, from left to right: Xiaopeng Li, Weilin He, Liqiang Shang, Meng Wang, Junjie Chen, Xiyue Zhang, Dong Wang, Yingquan Zhao.

Bristol, UK
Finished EPSRC Project RA

Today officially marks the completion of my Research Assistant role in the EPSRC-funded project: Exploring Barriers to AI Doctoral Studies. I have experienced the challenges and charm of interdisciplinary research. Collaborating with people from various countries and gaining my first in-depth understanding of the UK's AI PhD education system gave me quite a bit of culture shock. A huge thank you to everyone in our project team for their hard work and dedication!

It felt incredibly magical that my first visiting exchange as a PhD student was returning to my alma mater, Tianjin University, to visit The Wise Software Engineering Lab from Tianjin University. Sep 2025
Tianjin & Bristol
Graduated from Tianjin University & University of Bristol

Participating in the 3+1 AMD Programmes made 2025 a chaotic yet extraordinarily fulfilling year. Thanks to this program, I completed all my undergraduate credits within the first three years, and successfully managed my master's studies alongside writing both my bachelor's and master's dissertations in the fourth year. It was a period of immense academic pressure, but the relentless learning pushed me to my limits and enriched me deeply.

I am deeply grateful for my parents' continuous support. Now that everything is steadily falling into place, my heart is full of gratitude. My only regret this year is missing both my undergraduate and master's graduation ceremonies. Here is to making a wish: I hope to successfully earn my PhD and finally attend the graduation ceremony I've been longing for!

I still remember during my PhD interview, someone had written on the blackboard by the door: "Don't do AI! It'S, BAD!!! You will regret I". I wonder how I will answer this statement four years from now.
